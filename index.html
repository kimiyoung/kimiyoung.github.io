Short Bio

I am a 4th year undergraduate student from the Department of Computer Science, Tsinghua University. My research interests include computational biology, data mining and machine learning. I worked with Prof. Jie Tang on data mining and machine learning, and more specifically, active learning for networked data. I visited Stanford University in the summer of 2014 and worked with Prof. David L. Dill on computational biology, and more specifically, acute myeloid leukemia outcome prediction.

I am applying for PhD programs.

Research Experience





Knowledge Engineering Group, Tsinghua University

Advisor: Jie Tang, 2012/11 - present

Active Learning on Graphical Models

  • Designed active learning algorithms based on graphical models for social network mining. Built a connection between non-progressive diffusion model and active learning on graphs. Proved the hardness, correctness, convergence and approximation ratio of the proposed algorithm.
  • Our work is published at WSDM'2014. [PDF]

    • Modeling Streaming Networked Data

  • Studied active learning with streaming networked data. Proposed a framework for learning streaming networked data with active query strategy and network sampling techniques.
  • Studied online learning for graphical models to handle streaming networked data.
  • Researched on undirected graphical models with dual decomposition to speed up the learning process, in order to handle large amount of streaming data.
  • Our work is published at CIKM'2014. [PDF]

    • Social Network Analysis

  • Developed the large scale Social Analytic Engine (SAE) based on parallel vertex programs with integration of knowledge based and expert search systems. Leveraged cross indexing, memory mapping techniques to speed up the system. [PDF]
  • Studied emotion prediction on Tencent Weibo. Based on more than 3M tweets across 3 months and Hadoop distribued clusters. Built graphical models to model social influence and accurately predict user emotions.


    • Computational Biology Group, Stanford University

    Advisor: David L. Dill, 2014/06 - 2014/08

    Acute Myeloid Leukemia Outcome Prediction

  • Built a statistical model for predicting cancer outcome with clinical features protein arrays.
  • Proposed using dictionary learning for sparse coding to learn the latent representation of the Reverse Phase Protein Array (RPPA).
  • Identified important latent states from the RPPA data, which have both biological implications about signaling pathway regulation and statistical power for remission prediction.
  • Extracted Boolean rules by Boolean implications and confidence analysis.
  • Proposed a hybird feature selection algorithm for avoiding mixing up continuous and categorical features and achieved significant improvements over the datasets.
  • We won DREAM9 AML outcome prediction challenges #1 and #2. [Link]
